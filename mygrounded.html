SPCA

"Did you know that we receive almost no government funding? Everything we do depends on the generosity of people like you.

It costs nearly $40 million a year to take care of the tens of thousands of animals that need our help each year. With less than one percent government funding, we rely upon the generosity of the community to continue our life saving work.

Your donations help us to rescue, rehabilitate and re-home thousands of animals in need every year. As well as a one-off donation, regular donations and leaving a gift in your will, there are many other ways you can support New Zealand's animals.""

Red Cross

"Disasters, both natural and man-made, can happen at any time, anywhere in the world. People who never expected it can find themselves in urgent need of shelter, drinking water or medical aid. You can make sure that New Zealand Red Cross is always there to help when disaster strikes, bringing emergency supplies, medical care and relief to stricken families and communities.

By supporting New Zealand Red Cross, you'll be helping people in so many ways:

- refugee settlement - help give a warm welcome to former refugees so they feel part of their new community in Aotearoa

- disaster readiness, response and recovery - provide regularly training so our volunteers can respond to disasters as soon as they happen and stay through the long-term recovery stage

- international aid - support our international delegates to provide much-needed expertise in situations of conflict and disaster

- community programmes - wherever you are, we're there too, in plain sight or behind the scenes, supporting vulnerable people in your community"

Salvation Army

"More than ever, The Salvation Army depends on the generosity of our supporters to provide essential social services for those struggling to meet basic needs in these tough economic times.

We help over 120,000 New Zealanders every year - with budgeting advice, food parcels, life skills programmes and other comfort and support. Our aim is to give people a helping hand toward a brighter future.""
